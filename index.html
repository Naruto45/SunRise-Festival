Executive Summary

Sunrise Fairs is a new nonprofit initiative that produces free or low-cost, solar-powered pop-up fairs to teach sustainability and STEAM‚Äîintentionally centering youth arts education and community resilience. We will operate county-wide with a first fair slated for Auburn, WA in March 2026 and a broader programming runway beginning January 2026.

Events combine interactive workshops, art-making, green-tech demonstrations, and a marketplace featuring local creators and climate-forward entrepreneurs. Our focus is on underserved families and schools in King County who face cost, transportation, or opportunity barriers to STEAM and cultural learning.

The requested funding supports a three-year growth plan to deliver multiple fairs annually, build school and nonprofit partnerships, and establish a repeatable model. Budget plan: $180,000 over 3 years ($60,000/yr) managed with transparent milestones and outcomes.

Vision: A resilient, creative King County where families‚Äîespecially those historically underserved‚Äîengage in hands-on STEAM and arts learning that inspires climate literacy, career curiosity, and civic pride.

Community Need: Many families experience cost, transportation, and access barriers to enrichment. Program Model

Each fair includes three braided experiences:

  1. Solar-Powered Showcase: Demonstrations of off-grid energy, mobile solar stations, and hands-on sustainability exhibits.
  2. STEAM + Arts Workshops: Youth-friendly sessions (e.g., solar art, circuits & wearables, climate storytelling, maker stations) with a clear learning outcome and take-home artifact.
  3. Green Marketplace: Tables featuring local artists, cultural groups, and climate-forward vendors; micro-stages for student showcases and community performances.

Accessibility supports include transit-friendly site selection, ADA-aware layout, multilingual signage, and community ambassador roles for outreach and wayfinding.

Partnerships, Outreach & Access

Outreach Channels: 4Culture events (Hello 4Culture, Doors Open Share Outs, Launch Outreach), community radio and newsletters, multilingual flyers, faith/community hubs, and youth leadership councils. Community ambassadors will support door-to-door flyering and event-day wayfinding.

Access Commitments: Transit-first sites; ADA-aware layouts; ASL/interpretation as needed; childcare corner at larger events; sliding-scale for vendor fees; reserved free workshop seats for Title I schools.
